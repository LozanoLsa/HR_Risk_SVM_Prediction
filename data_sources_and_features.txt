PROJECT: HR_Risk_Analytics
TITLE: Data Sources & Feature Engineering

--------------------------------------------------
OVERVIEW:
This dataset integrates workforce performance, operational impact, and organizational variables from an industrial environment.

The objective is to identify employees with high operational risk by linking human behavior with productivity, scrap, and stability indicators.

--------------------------------------------------
DATA SOURCES AND VARIABLE ORIGIN:

1. TRAINING AND DEVELOPMENT RECORDS
Variables:
- training_hours_annual

Taken from:
Employee training and development logs.

Reason:
Training influences skill level, adaptability, and overall performance consistency.

--------------------------------------------------
2. ATTENDANCE AND DISCIPLINE SYSTEM
Variables:
- punctuality_rate

Taken from:
Attendance and time tracking systems.

Reason:
Punctuality reflects discipline and reliability, directly impacting operational flow.

--------------------------------------------------
3. PERFORMANCE AND OPERATIONS DATA
Variables:
- productivity_index
- scrap_associated_pct

Taken from:
Production performance systems and quality tracking linked to operator activity.

Reason:
These variables connect employee performance with operational outcomes such as efficiency and defect generation.

--------------------------------------------------
4. ENGAGEMENT AND WORKFORCE ASSESSMENT
Variables:
- engagement_score

Taken from:
Internal engagement surveys or evaluation systems.

Reason:
Low engagement is often associated with reduced performance, higher variability, and increased risk.

--------------------------------------------------
5. EXPERIENCE AND ROTATION
Variables:
- experience_yrs
- area_rotation_rate

Taken from:
HR records and workforce movement tracking.

Reason:
Experience stabilizes performance, while frequent rotation may increase variability and learning curve effects.

--------------------------------------------------
6. ORGANIZATIONAL CONTEXT
Variables:
- department
- shift
- contract_type

Taken from:
HR master data and workforce assignment systems.

Reason:
Different departments, shifts, and contract types operate under varying conditions and constraints.

--------------------------------------------------
7. TARGET VARIABLE (RISK CLASSIFICATION)
Variables:
- high_risk

Taken from:
Derived classification based on performance, variability, and operational impact.

Reason:
Indicates whether the employee represents a higher operational risk.

--------------------------------------------------
FEATURE ENGINEERING LOGIC:

The dataset combines human behavior, operational performance, and organizational context to identify patterns associated with workforce risk.

Each variable contributes to understanding how people influence process outcomes.

--------------------------------------------------
BUSINESS LOGIC:

Operational risk is not only driven by machines or processes.

Human factors such as engagement, experience, discipline, and workload conditions directly impact productivity, scrap, and stability.

--------------------------------------------------
GOAL:

Identify high-risk employees to:
- Improve productivity and operational stability
- Reduce scrap associated with human factors
- Support workforce development decisions
- Improve retention and performance management

--------------------------------------------------
NOTE:

Data originates from an industrial environment, integrating HR systems, production data, and quality tracking to connect workforce behavior with operational performance.
